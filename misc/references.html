HealthIT. gov - Why EHRs or electronic health records?

This source gives a clear outline of the benefits of EHR and a record of obstacles likely to be faced in the process.



National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST):

NIST 800-63 – Security of Electronic Health Records

Health care providers increasingly use mobile devices to receive, store, process, and transmit patient clinical information.

NZ Electronic Health record strategy

This Review specifically compares the current EHR approach with the scope and potential benefits that may be delivered by a ‘Single’ EHR system and provides independent advice on the options for delivering a greater level of universality through an EHR in New Zealand.



Journal of Medical Ethics - Article on "Ethical Challenges in Electronic Health Records”

Finding what has already been written on a particular subject is known as a Literature Review. The next article reviews the ethical issues related to EHRs mainly privacy, consent, and equity.



News and Updates / HIPAA Compliance Guide:

HIPAA Journal enlightens the user on the legal cost and some ethical issues in issues of patient’s data privacy and security in records.



WHO guidelines to digital health: Digital Health Guidelines

Contemporary recommendations that have been provided by WHO regarding the use of technology in the field of health care and the possibilities for the use of new technologies presented by digital health.
